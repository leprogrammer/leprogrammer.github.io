About Me

I am a Computer Science and Applied Mathematics & Statistics Double Major from Stony Brook University with a 3.69 culmulative GPA. I graduated in May 2018 with a Bachelors of Science, finishing both majors in 3 years. Some of my interests include teaching, working with robots, learning new programming languages, and playing soccer or card games.

Experience

kidOYO Mentor   |   September 2016 - May 2018

I joined the Noizivy organization in September 2016 as an intern and had the opportunity of taking on the unique challenge of teaching programming languages to children aged 8 to 16. Starting in the spring semester of 2017, I moved up from an intern and took up a position as a mentor for the kidOYO program. As part of my responsibilities I created a five week Intro to Robotics course using an Arduino Microcontroller as a starting point. During the summer of 2017, I continued working with the organization for their Summer Camp and taught an introductory Python and JavaFX session. For my work for the organization, I was one of the recipients of the Arie E. Kaufman Mentor Service Award for the Fall 2017 and Spring 2018 semesters.

Commuter Assistant   |   May 2017 - May 2018

I was selected into the Commuter Assistant Program under the Office of Commuter Student Services and Off Campus Living in May 2017. As a Commuter Assistant, I helped new students become better aquainted to Stony Brook University. This position has allowed me to continue developing my communication and public speaking skills.

Computer Science Undergraduate Teaching Assistant   |   May 2016 - August 2016

I was selected by Professor Ahmad Esmaili to be one of his Undergraduate Teaching Assistants for CSE114 and to be the only one for CSE214. As a Teaching Assistant, I was able to demonstrate my understanding of the foundations of Java and Data Structures. This position also first introduced me to the experience of teaching Computer Science and programming which has now become one of my passions.
